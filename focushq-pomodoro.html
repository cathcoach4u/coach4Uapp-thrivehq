Pomodoro Timer

Work in focused sprints. Rest on purpose. Get more done.

Work
25:00
Round 1 of 4
How it works
  1. Choose one task to focus on before you start.
  2. Start the 25-minute timer and work only on that task.
  3. When the timer ends, take a 5-minute break — step away from the screen.
  4. After 4 rounds, take a longer break of 15–30 minutes.
Tips
  • Put your phone out of reach for the 25 minutes.
  • If a distraction pops into your head, jot it down and come back to it later.
  • The break is part of the technique — don't skip it.
  • Short tasks? Stack a few into one pomodoro.
Named after a tomato-shaped kitchen timer (pomodoro = Italian for tomato). Developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s.
